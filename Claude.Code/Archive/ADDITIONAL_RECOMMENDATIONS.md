## COMMON CLIENT OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### "That's too expensive"
**Don't Say:** "I can come down..."
**Do Say:** "I understand budget is a factor. Let's talk about what the cost of NOT solving this problem is. How much revenue are you losing without a professional brand and lead generation system? Most clients see ROI within 3-6 months. Would a payment plan help?"

### "I need to think about it"
**Don't Say:** "Okay, let me know!"
**Do Say:** "Absolutely, this is an important decision. What specific concerns do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or something about the approach I suggested?"

### "Can I just get a logo?"
**Don't Say:** "Sure!"
**Do Say:** "I could do that, but here's what I've seen happen: People get a logo, then realize they need colors, fonts, a website, social templates... and end up spending more piecing it together than if they'd done it right from the start. Plus, a logo without strategy is just decoration. Can I show you what a complete foundation looks like?"

### "My nephew can do this cheaper"
**Don't Say:** "Well, you get what you pay for"
**Do Say:** "That's great that you have someone willing to help! The difference is I'm building a business system, not just making things look pretty. I'm setting up automation that generates leads while you sleep, creating brand strategy that positions you as the expert, and building systems you own. Your nephew might be able to design a logo, but can he build you a client acquisition machine?"

### "I don't understand all this automation stuff"
**Don't Say:** [Technical explanation]
**Do Say:** "That's exactly why I'm here. You don't need to understand how it works - you need to understand what it does for you. Imagine if every person who visited your website got automatically added to your CRM, received a welcome email series, and got reminders to book a call with you - all without you lifting a finger. That's automation. Want me to show you how it works in plain English?"

## SELF-CARE FOR SOLOPRENEURS

### Don't Burn Out (You've Been Through Enough):

**Daily Non-Negotiables:**
- [ ] 7-8 hours sleep
- [ ] Meals (not working through lunch)
- [ ] Move your body (walk, yoga, whatever)
- [ ] Set work hours (even if from home)
- [ ] Time with kids without phone

**Weekly Practices:**
- [ ] One full day off (actually off)
- [ ] Something just for you (not business)
- [ ] Connect with friends/family
- [ ] Review wins, not just to-dos
- [ ] Plan next week proactively

**Monthly Check-Ins:**
- [ ] Assess burnout level (1-10 scale)
- [ ] Adjust if needed (raise prices, say no, get help)
- [ ] Celebrate progress
- [ ] Review financials
- [ ] Plan next month's focus

**Remember:**
- You can't pour from an empty cup
- This business is supposed to give you freedom, not trap you
- Making less money with sanity > making more while miserable
- Your kids need a present mom more than a wealthy one
- You're rebuilding for the long haul, not a sprint

## QUESTIONS TO ASK EVERY QUARTER

### Business Health Check:

**Financial:**
1. Am I making enough to support myself/family?
2. Are profit margins healthy (30%+ after expenses)?
3. Is cash flow positive and predictable?
4. Am I pricing appropriately (or leaving money on table)?

**Operational:**
1. Are my systems actually saving me time?
2. What's taking too long that I could automate/delegate?
3. Am I working too many hours?
4. What am I doing that someone else could do?

**Marketing:**
1. Where are my best leads coming from?
2. What content is performing best?
3. Are partnerships generating business?
4. Is my positioning still resonant?

**Client Satisfaction:**
1. Are clients happy (ask them!)?
2. Am I getting referrals?
3. Are there recurring complaints/issues?
4. Would I want to work with more clients like my current ones?

**Personal:**
1. Am I enjoying this?
2. Am I proud of what I'm building?
3. Do I have time for what matters?
4. Where am I headed long-term?
