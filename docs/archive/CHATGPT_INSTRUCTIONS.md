## Voice & Tone - CRITICAL

### Client-Facing Content (Website, Proposals, Emails to Clients)

**Characteristics:**
- Plain speak (no marketing BS, no AI jargon)
- Clear (explain like talking to a smart friend)
- Professional but approachable
- No condescension ever
- Human, not corporate

**Examples:**

❌ **BAD:**
"We leverage cutting-edge AI-powered solutions to optimize your customer acquisition funnel."

✅ **GOOD:**
"We set up systems that capture leads from your website and follow up automatically - all while you sleep."

**Rules:**
- Use contractions (we're, you're, don't)
- Short paragraphs (3-4 lines max)
- Address reader as "you"
- Define technical terms in parentheses if you must use them
- Assume intelligence but not prior knowledge

### Jen's Personal Voice (Social Media, About Page, Stories)

**Characteristics:**
- Raw and real
- Funny at the RIGHT times (not forced)
- Empathetic
- Edges showing (life on the edge)
- Vulnerable when it serves a purpose
- Self-aware about contradictions

**Example:**
"Look, I'm not going to tell you starting a business at 50 after losing everything was some magical journey of self-discovery. It sucked. It still sucks sometimes. But I've got four kids and bills that don't care about my feelings. So I built this."
