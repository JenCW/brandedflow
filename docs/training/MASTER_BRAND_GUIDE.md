## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice: The Professional + Real Balance

**Core Principle:**
Professional presentation on the outside because I care about perception. Raw and real on the inside because that's who I am.

### Client-Facing Voice (All External Content)

**Characteristics:**
1. **Plain speak** - No marketing BS, no AI jargon
2. **Clear** - Explain things like talking to a smart friend
3. **Helpful** - Include tips explaining marketing terms and actions
4. **Professional** - Well-written, organized, thoughtful
5. **Human** - Written by a person, for people

**What This Sounds Like:**

‚ùå **BAD (Marketing BS):**
"We leverage cutting-edge AI-powered solutions to optimize your customer acquisition funnel and maximize ROI through omnichannel automation strategies."

‚úÖ **GOOD (Plain Speak):**
"We set up automated systems that capture leads from your website, send them helpful emails, and book them on your calendar‚Äîall while you sleep."

‚ùå **BAD (Jargon):**
"Our CRM integration facilitates seamless data flow across your tech stack."

‚úÖ **GOOD (Clear):**
"We connect your contact form to your email system so you never lose a lead."

**Rules for Client-Facing Content:**
- Use contractions (we're, you're, don't)
- Short paragraphs (3-4 lines max)
- Break up text with bullets and headers
- Address reader as "you"
- Tell stories and use examples
- Define any necessary technical terms in parentheses
- Assume intelligence but not prior knowledge

### Jen's Personal Voice (Social Media, About Page, Personal Stories)

**Characteristics:**
- Raw and real
- Funny at the RIGHT times
- Empathetic
- Edges showing (life on the edge)
- God-loving but not preachy
- Vulnerable when it serves a purpose
- Self-aware about contradictions

**What This Sounds Like:**

"Look, I'm not going to tell you starting a business at 50 after losing everything was some magical journey of self-discovery. It sucked. It still sucks sometimes. But I've got four kids, two dogs, and bills that don't care about my feelings. So I built this. And if I can help you not drown in the same marketing BS that had me confused for months, then at least something good came from all this chaos."

**Or:**

"People ask me how I handle sales calls with my phone anxiety. The answer? I don't. I built a whole damn business around NOT having to answer the phone. That's why the AI receptionist thing is chef's kiss for me. You can run a serious business and never have to answer a sales call. Revolutionary."

**Rules for Personal Content:**
- Be real about struggles
- Own the contradictions
- Humor is allowed (and encouraged at the right times)
- Vulnerability builds trust
- Don't overshare just to overshare (must serve a purpose)
- Make it relatable to their struggles
- Always tie back to how this helps them

### Tone by Context

**Educational Content (Blog Posts, Tutorials, How-Tos):**
- Tone: Patient, clear, encouraging
- Goal: Make complex things simple
- Example: "Let me break down automation in plain English. It's just having your computer do the repetitive stuff so you don't have to. Like this..."

**Sales Content (Service Pages, Proposals):**
- Tone: Confident but not pushy, honest, value-focused
- Goal: Show ROI without hype
- Example: "Here's what happens when you piece together branding yourself: You spend $500 on a logo, then realize you need colors, fonts, templates, a website... and you're at $5,000 anyway. We do it all together for less."

**Support Content (Client Emails, Check-ins):**
- Tone: Supportive, responsive, solution-oriented
- Goal: Make clients feel heard and helped
- Example: "I totally get wanting the colors just right. Let me send you three variations and we'll adjust until it feels perfect."

**Social Media (LinkedIn, Instagram):**
